## 📺 Topic 1: MEDIA

### 🔹 Step 1 — 5 Rich Words + Collocations

| # | Word | Meaning | Collocation / Usage |
|---|------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Manipulate** | to influence unfairly | `manipulate public opinion` / `manipulate facts` / `manipulate emotions` |
| 2 | **Biased** | showing unfair preference | `biased reporting` / `biased coverage` / `biased view` |
| 3 | **Credible** | trustworthy | `credible source` / `credible news` / `credible evidence` |
| 4 | **Sensationalize** | to exaggerate for drama | `sensationalize news` / `sensationalize events` / `sensationalize stories` |
| 5 | **Mass media** | widespread communication | `influence of mass media` / `mass media coverage` |

### 🔹 Step 2 — 5 Semantic Sentences

```text
1. Manipulate     → Some newspapers manipulate public opinion through biased headlines.
2. Biased         → Viewers should avoid channels known for biased reporting.
3. Credible       → Always verify information from multiple credible sources.
4. Sensationalize → Tabloid media often sensationalize minor events to attract readers.
5. Mass media     → Mass media plays a powerful role in shaping modern society.
```

### 🔹 Step 3 — Storytelling (Easy → Big Sentence)

**5 Easy Sentences:**
- Ayan reads the news online.
- He checks many websites.
- Some stories seem fake.
- He compares different sources.
- He learns the real truth.

**🔥 Big Band 7+ Sentence:**
> *Ayan, who reads news online every morning, has learned to identify biased reporting and avoid outlets that sensationalize events, preferring instead credible sources that do not manipulate facts — a habit that protects him from the misleading influence of mass media.*

### 🔹 Step 4 — 7 Sentence Structure Variants

```markdown
1. Simple            → Ayan reads the news daily.
2. Compound          → Some reports are biased, but Ayan checks multiple sources.
3. Complex           → Because media can manipulate opinions, Ayan verifies every story.
4. Compound-Complex  → Although mass media is powerful, Ayan stays informed, and he chooses credible outlets.
5. Conditional       → If people only trusted credible sources, misinformation would decrease.
6. Relative Clause   → Ayan, who loves journalism, avoids channels that sensationalize stories.
7. Participle Phrase → Frustrated by fake news, Ayan began reading international newspapers.
```

## 🎭 Topic 2: CULTURE

### 🔹 Step 1 — 5 Rich Words + Collocations

| # | Word | Meaning | Collocation / Usage |
|---|------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Heritage** | traditions passed down | `cultural heritage` / `rich heritage` / `preserve heritage` |
| 2 | **Diverse** | varied, different | `diverse culture` / `diverse population` / `diverse traditions` |
| 3 | **Preserve** | to protect from loss | `preserve traditions` / `preserve language` / `preserve history` |
| 4 | **Embrace** | to accept warmly | `embrace diversity` / `embrace change` / `embrace culture` |
| 5 | **Customs** | traditional practices | `local customs` / `follow customs` / `respect customs` |

### 🔹 Step 2 — 5 Semantic Sentences

```text
1. Heritage → Bangladesh has a rich cultural heritage dating back thousands of years.
2. Diverse  → India is known for its diverse traditions and languages.
3. Preserve → We must preserve our native languages for future generations.
4. Embrace  → Young people should embrace both tradition and modernity.
5. Customs  → Every country has unique customs that reflect its history.
```

### 🔹 Step 3 — Storytelling (Easy → Big Sentence)

**5 Easy Sentences:**
- Mim visited her village.
- She saw old traditions.
- Her grandmother told stories.
- She learned local customs.
- She felt proud of her culture.

**🔥 Big Band 7+ Sentence:**
> *Mim, who recently visited her grandmother's village, embraced the diverse customs of her community, listened to ancient stories that preserve her family's cultural heritage, and returned home deeply proud of her roots.*

### 🔹 Step 4 — 7 Sentence Structure Variants

```markdown
1. Simple            → Mim visited her village.
2. Compound          → Mim learned new customs, and she felt proud.
3. Complex           → Although she lives in the city, Mim deeply respects her heritage.
4. Compound-Complex  → Because she loves her roots, Mim visits her village often, and she embraces its traditions.
5. Conditional       → If we don't preserve our culture, it will disappear with time.
6. Relative Clause   → Mim, who comes from a traditional family, loves the customs that shaped her.
7. Participle Phrase → Inspired by her grandmother's stories, Mim decided to preserve her heritage.
```

## 📝 Mini Paragraph (Combined)

> *In today's interconnected world, **mass media** plays a powerful role in shaping how people perceive different cultures. While **credible** journalism can help audiences appreciate **diverse** traditions and **preserve** global **heritage**, **biased** outlets often **sensationalize** cultural differences and **manipulate** public opinion. Therefore, individuals must critically evaluate the information they consume and actively **embrace** the rich **customs** of communities around the world to build a more tolerant society.*
