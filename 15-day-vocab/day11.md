## 🚨 Topic 1: CRIME

### 🔹 Step 1 — 5 Rich Words + Collocations

| # | Word | Meaning | Collocation / Usage |
|---|------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Deter** | to discourage / prevent | `deter criminals` / `deter crime` / `act as a deterrent` |
| 2 | **Rehabilitate** | to reform a criminal | `rehabilitate offenders` / `rehabilitation program` |
| 3 | **Lenient** | too soft, not strict | `lenient sentence` / `lenient punishment` / `lenient judge` |
| 4 | **Perpetrator** | person who commits crime | `catch the perpetrator` / `identify the perpetrator` |
| 5 | **Incarcerate** | to put in prison | `incarcerate criminals` / `incarcerated for years` |

### 🔹 Step 2 — 5 Semantic Sentences

```text
1. Deter         → Harsh penalties are often used to deter criminals from reoffending.
2. Rehabilitate  → Modern prisons aim to rehabilitate offenders rather than just punish them.
3. Lenient       → Many citizens feel that judges are too lenient with young criminals.
4. Perpetrator   → Police worked tirelessly to identify the perpetrator of the robbery.
5. Incarcerate   → Governments spend millions each year to incarcerate repeat offenders.
```

### 🔹 Step 3 — Storytelling (Easy → Big Sentence)

**5 Easy Sentences:**
- A thief robbed a shop.
- The police caught him.
- The judge gave a small punishment.
- People were angry.
- The law should be stricter.

**🔥 Big Band 7+ Sentence:**
> *After the perpetrator of a shocking robbery was caught by the police, many citizens were outraged by the lenient sentence he received, arguing that stricter laws are needed to deter future crimes and that repeat offenders should be incarcerated rather than simply rehabilitated.*

### 🔹 Step 4 — 7 Sentence Structure Variants

```markdown
1. Simple            → The police caught the thief.
2. Compound          → The perpetrator was arrested, and the judge sentenced him.
3. Complex           → Although the crime was serious, the sentence was lenient.
4. Compound-Complex  → Because the laws are weak, crime continues, and citizens demand reform.
5. Conditional       → If punishments were stricter, they would deter more criminals.
6. Relative Clause   → The perpetrator, who committed the robbery, was finally incarcerated.
7. Participle Phrase → Determined to reduce crime, the government launched new rehabilitation programs.
```

## 🏛️ Topic 2: GOVERNMENT

### 🔹 Step 1 — 5 Rich Words + Collocations

| # | Word | Meaning | Collocation / Usage |
|---|------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Implement** | to put into action | `implement policies` / `implement reforms` / `implement laws` |
| 2 | **Transparent** | open and honest | `transparent government` / `transparent process` |
| 3 | **Accountability** | responsibility for actions | `public accountability` / `demand accountability` |
| 4 | **Legislation** | laws passed by government | `pass legislation` / `strict legislation` / `new legislation` |
| 5 | **Bureaucracy** | complex official system | `government bureaucracy` / `reduce bureaucracy` |

### 🔹 Step 2 — 5 Semantic Sentences

```text
1. Implement       → The government plans to implement new education reforms next year.
2. Transparent     → A transparent government builds public trust and confidence.
3. Accountability  → Public officials must be held to strict accountability standards.
4. Legislation     → New legislation has been passed to protect workers' rights.
5. Bureaucracy     → Excessive bureaucracy often slows down important decisions.
```

### 🔹 Step 3 — Storytelling (Easy → Big Sentence)

**5 Easy Sentences:**
- The government made new rules.
- People wanted honest leaders.
- Officials had to be responsible.
- Some processes were slow.
- Reforms were needed.

**🔥 Big Band 7+ Sentence:**
> *When the government decided to implement sweeping reforms, citizens demanded greater transparency and accountability from their leaders, arguing that new legislation was necessary to reduce bureaucracy and restore public trust in the political system.*

### 🔹 Step 4 — 7 Sentence Structure Variants

```markdown
1. Simple            → The government implemented new laws.
2. Compound          → Citizens demanded accountability, and officials responded.
3. Complex           → Although bureaucracy slows progress, reforms are still possible.
4. Compound-Complex  → Because transparency matters, the government changed its policies, and trust improved.
5. Conditional       → If legislation is strict, corruption will decrease significantly.
6. Relative Clause   → The government, which recently passed new legislation, promised more reforms.
7. Participle Phrase → Frustrated by bureaucracy, citizens demanded transparent leadership.
```

## 📝 Mini Paragraph (Combined)

> *Maintaining law and order is one of the most important duties of any **government**. To effectively **deter** crime, authorities must **implement** strict **legislation** while ensuring full **transparency** and **accountability** within the justice system. When the **perpetrator** of a crime is caught, courts must avoid being too **lenient** — instead, offenders should either be **incarcerated** for serious crimes or placed in programs designed to **rehabilitate** them. Moreover, reducing excessive **bureaucracy** would allow reforms to reach citizens faster and build stronger public trust.*

📊 **Word count:** ~90 words | Uses all 10 words ✅ | Mixed structures ✅
