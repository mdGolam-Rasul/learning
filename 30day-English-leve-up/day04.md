# 📚 Section 1: Grammar — Future Tense + Conditionals

## 🔮 Future Tense — 3 Types

---

## ✅ Type 1: Simple Future — `will`

### Use

Simple Future is used for:

- Future actions
- Instant decisions
- Predictions
- Promises

### Formula

```text
Subject + will + Base Verb
```

### Examples

```text
I will visit my grandparents next week.
She will become a doctor someday.
It will rain tomorrow.
We will help you.
```

---

## ✅ Type 2: Going To — Planned Future

### Use

`Going to` is used for plans that were decided before speaking.

### Formula

```text
Subject + am/is/are + going to + Base Verb
```

### Examples

```text
I am going to study abroad next year.
They are going to build a new house.
She is going to start her own business.
```

---

## ✅ Type 3: Future Continuous

### Use

Future Continuous is used for an action that will be happening at a specific time in the future.

### Formula

```text
Subject + will be + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

```text
This time tomorrow, I will be flying to London.
At 8 PM tonight, she will be studying.
Next year, we will be living in a new city.
```

---

# ⚡ Conditionals — IF Sentences

Conditionals are essential for Band 7+ speaking and writing.

---

## ✅ Conditional Type 1 — Real Future

### Use

Possible future situations.

### Formula

```text
If + Simple Present, Subject + will + Base Verb
```

### Examples

```text
If I study hard, I will pass the exam.
If it rains, we will stay home.
If you help me, I will be grateful.
```

---

## ✅ Conditional Type 2 — Imaginary / Hypothetical

### Use

Imaginary situations in the present or future.

### Formula

```text
If + Simple Past, Subject + would + Base Verb
```

### Examples

```text
If I had a million dollars, I would travel the world.
If I were you, I would accept the offer.
If she knew English, she would get a better job.
```

---

## ✅ Conditional Type 3 — Past Imaginary

### Use

Imaginary situations in the past.

### Formula

```text
If + Past Perfect, Subject + would have + Past Participle
```

### Examples

```text
If I had studied harder, I would have passed.
If he had arrived on time, he would have caught the train.
```

---

## 🔥 Conditionals in Storytelling

Simple:

```text
I didn't have money. I couldn't buy a car.
```

Band 7+:

```text
If I had had enough money, I would have bought a car last year.
```

Simple:

```text
I want to visit Europe.
```

Band 7+:

```text
If I had the opportunity, I would definitely visit Europe and immerse myself in its rich culture.
```

# 🎬 Section 3: Storytelling — Action Expansion

## 🎯 Today's Focus: Action Expansion

Action means what happened in the story and what the character did.

Strong action description makes your writing and speaking more dynamic.

---

## 🎨 5 Levels of Action Expansion

### Original Simple Action

```text
I ran.
```

### Level 1: Add How

```text
I ran quickly.
```

### Level 2: Add Where + Why

```text
I ran quickly to catch the bus because I was late.
```

### Level 3: Add Emotion + Details

```text
My heart pounding, I ran quickly through the busy streets to catch the bus because I was already late for my meeting.
```

### Level 4: Rich Vocabulary + Participles

```text
With my heart pounding furiously, I sprinted through the bustling streets, desperately trying to catch the departing bus as I was running late for an important meeting.
```

### Level 5: Band 8+ Action Description

```text
With my heart pounding furiously against my chest, I sprinted through the bustling streets of Dhaka, my bag bouncing wildly on my shoulder, desperately trying to catch the departing bus. Sweat was trickling down my forehead, but I refused to give up, knowing that being late for this crucial meeting would have serious consequences.
```

---

## 🔑 Action Expansion Formula

```text
BASIC:
Subject + Verb

RICH:
Emotion/State + Subject + Rich Verb + How/Manner + Where + Why + Consequence
```

---

# 🎤 Section 5: Speaking Practice — Future Dreams

## IELTS Part 1 Questions About Future

```text
What are your plans for the future?
Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
Do you have any travel plans?
What job would you like to have?
```

---

## Band 7+ Template

```text
[INTRODUCTION]
I have several exciting plans for my future, and I'm incredibly passionate about achieving them.

[SHORT-TERM PLAN]
In the next year or two, I'm going to _____. I believe this will _____. I'm currently _____.

[LONG-TERM PLAN]
Looking further ahead, I envision myself _____. Five years from now, I will be _____. I'm particularly interested in _____ because _____.

[CONDITIONAL HOPE]
If everything goes according to plan, I will _____. However, if I had the chance, I would also _____.

[CONCLUSION]
Overall, I'm optimistic about my future and determined to work hard to achieve my aspirations.
```

---

## Sample Band 7+ Answer

```text
I have several exciting plans for my future, and I'm incredibly passionate about achieving them.

In the next year or two, I'm going to complete my undergraduate degree in Computer Science. I believe this will equip me with the cutting-edge skills necessary to thrive in today's competitive tech industry. I'm currently striving to enhance my programming abilities and gain expertise in emerging technologies.

Looking further ahead, I envision myself working as a software engineer at a reputable multinational company. Five years from now, I will be leading innovative projects that harness artificial intelligence to transform people's lives. I'm particularly interested in this field because technology has become indispensable in modern society.

If everything goes according to plan, I will have achieved significant milestones. However, if I had the opportunity, I would also love to pursue higher studies abroad, which would broaden my horizons immensely.

Overall, I'm genuinely optimistic about my future and absolutely determined to work diligently toward accomplishing my aspirations.
```

# 📊 Section 7: IELTS Writing Task 1 Body Paragraphs

## Body Paragraph Structure

You have already learned:

```text
Introduction
Overview
```

Today you will learn:

```text
Body Paragraph 1
Body Paragraph 2
```

---

## Body Paragraph Formula

```text
Body 1:
Biggest / most significant data + specific numbers

Body 2:
Comparison / contrast + remaining data
```

---

## Trend Vocabulary

| Increase | Decrease | Stable | Fluctuate |
|---|---|---|---|
| rose sharply | plummeted | remained stable | fluctuated |
| surged | declined | leveled off | oscillated |
| climbed | dropped | stayed constant | varied |
| increased significantly | fell dramatically | plateaued | wavered |

---

## Comparison Vocabulary

```text
significantly higher than
marginally lower than
approximately the same as
twice as high as
half the amount of
in stark contrast to
```

---

## Numerical Expressions

```text
approximately 25%
just over 30%
nearly 50%
slightly less than 40%
a vast majority
a small proportion
```

---

## Sample Body Paragraphs

Question:

```text
The line graph shows the population of 3 cities A, B, and C from 2000 to 2020.
```

### Body 1

```text
Looking at City A, the population rose dramatically from 2 million in 2000 to approximately 8 million by 2020, representing a fourfold increase. This upward trend was particularly steep between 2010 and 2015, where it surged from 4 to 6 million.
```

### Body 2

```text
In contrast, City B experienced a more gradual growth, climbing from 3 million to just over 5 million during the same period. Meanwhile, City C's population remained relatively stable, fluctuating around 2 million throughout the two decades, with only a slight increase by 2020.
```
