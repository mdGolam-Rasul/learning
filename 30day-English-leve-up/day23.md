# 📚 Section 3: Environment Speaking Practice

## 🎤 Environment Part 1 Questions

### Question 1

**Do you think your country has environmental problems?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Unfortunately, yes. Bangladesh faces several serious environmental challenges. Dhaka’s air quality is often extremely poor, with high levels of pollution affecting public health. River contamination, especially in the Buriganga, has also damaged aquatic ecosystems. In addition, rising sea levels threaten coastal regions due to climate change. However, environmental awareness and government initiatives are gradually increasing.

---

### Question 2

**Do you recycle at home?**

### Band 8+ Answer

To be honest, I try my best to recycle, although recycling infrastructure is still developing in Bangladesh. At home, I separate paper, plastic, and organic waste whenever possible. I also try to reduce my carbon footprint by reusing bags, conserving water, and limiting plastic consumption. However, I believe systematic waste management policies are needed for real progress.

---

### Question 3

**Do you think people will become more environmentally conscious in the future?**

### Band 8+ Answer

I am cautiously optimistic. As climate change becomes more visible, public awareness is increasing. Younger generations, in particular, are promoting sustainability through social media campaigns and local movements. Many companies are also facing pressure to adopt eco-friendly practices. However, turning awareness into action is still challenging, especially in developing countries.

# 🎤 Environment Part 2 Cue Card

## Cue Card

You should say:

- What the problem is

---

## Sample Answer Structure

### Introduction

I would like to discuss one of the most serious environmental problems in Dhaka: severe air pollution. It has deeply affected the daily lives of millions of residents.

### What It Is

Air pollution in Dhaka has reached alarming levels. Toxic particulate matter, industrial emissions, and vehicle exhaust contaminate the atmosphere and create a gray haze over the city.

### Causes

Several factors contribute to this issue. Brick kilns release harmful greenhouse gases, old diesel vehicles emit pollutants, and construction dust worsens air quality. Deforestation and poor urban planning also reduce natural air purification.

### Effects

The effects are extremely harmful. Many people suffer from respiratory illnesses, asthma, and heart-related problems. Children and elderly people are especially vulnerable. Poor air quality also reduces overall quality of life.

### Solutions

Solving this problem requires stricter emission rules, better public transport, more renewable energy use, and green urban planning. Individuals can also help by using public transport, walking short distances, and reducing unnecessary fuel consumption.

### Conclusion

Dhaka’s air pollution crisis requires urgent action from both the government and citizens. Protecting the environment is essential for future generations.

# 🎤 Environment Part 3 Questions

## Question 1

**What are the main causes of environmental problems?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Environmental problems come from several interconnected causes. Rapid industrialization has released large amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, contributing to climate change. Unsustainable consumption has also increased resource depletion. In addition, deforestation, pollution, and overpopulation have damaged ecological balance. In Bangladesh, urban expansion has destroyed wetlands and reduced biodiversity. Overall, the main problem is the conflict between economic growth and environmental conservation.

---

## Question 2

**Who should be responsible for protecting the environment: individuals or governments?**

### Band 8+ Answer

I believe both individuals and governments are responsible. Governments must create strong policies, enforce emission standards, invest in renewable energy, and protect biodiversity. These large-scale changes cannot be achieved by individuals alone. However, individuals also matter because personal choices such as reducing plastic use, saving energy, and using public transport can collectively create a major impact. The best solution is cooperation between citizens and governments.

---

## Question 3

**How can countries balance economic development and environmental protection?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Countries can balance development and environmental protection by investing in green innovation. Developed countries should lead by using renewable energy and supporting cleaner technologies. Developing nations can adopt sustainable industries instead of repeating the polluting development path of richer countries. Green sectors such as eco-tourism, sustainable agriculture, and renewable energy can create jobs while protecting ecosystems. Long-term planning and international cooperation are essential.

# ✍️ Section 4: Task 2 Environment Essay Practice

## Essay Question

Many environmental problems are too big for individuals to tackle, while others argue that individual efforts can significantly impact environmental protection. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

---

## Planning

### View 1: Problems Are Too Big for Individuals

- Global scale
- Need systemic change
- Corporate responsibility
- Government policy required

### View 2: Individual Efforts Matter

- Collective power
- Lifestyle changes
- Local community action
- Consumer pressure

### Opinion

Both are needed, but individual action must be combined with government and corporate action.

---

## Band 8+ Sample Essay

In the face of escalating environmental crises, the debate over responsibility has intensified. While some people argue that environmental problems are too large for individuals to solve, others believe that personal efforts can meaningfully contribute to environmental protection. In my view, although these issues require systemic action, individual contributions remain essential for achieving environmental sustainability.

Supporters of the first view argue that environmental challenges such as climate change, deforestation, and ocean pollution are global in scale. Multinational corporations emit huge quantities of greenhouse gases, governments determine energy policies, and international agreements regulate emissions. Individual recycling or reduced plastic use, while helpful, may seem minor compared to industrial-scale contamination. Therefore, tackling these problems requires government intervention, corporate accountability, and international cooperation.

However, individual actions can collectively create significant change. When millions of people adopt sustainable practices, reduce their carbon footprint, support eco-friendly products, and advocate for environmental conservation, the combined effect can be powerful. Consumers also influence markets because demand for renewable energy, electric vehicles, and sustainable goods encourages companies to change their behavior. Grassroots movements can also pressure governments to introduce stronger environmental laws.

In conclusion, environmental problems are too complex to be solved by individuals alone, but personal action still plays a vital role. A combined approach involving government policies, corporate responsibility, and individual commitment offers the most effective path toward environmental sustainability.
