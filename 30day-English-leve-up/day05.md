# 📚 Section 1: Grammar — Articles + Prepositions

## 🔤 Articles: `a`, `an`, `the`

Articles are small words, but article mistakes can seriously affect IELTS writing and speaking accuracy.

---

## ✅ `A` vs `AN`

### Use `A`

Use **a** before a consonant sound.

```text
a book
a university
a European country
a one-way street
```

> `university` starts with a vowel letter, but the sound is “yu”, so we use **a university**.

---

### Use `AN`

Use **an** before a vowel sound.

```text
an apple
an hour
an honest man
an MBA
```

> `hour` has a silent `h`, so we say **an hour**.  
> `MBA` sounds like “em-bee-ay”, so we say **an MBA**.

---

## ✅ When to Use `THE`

Use **the** for specific, unique, or already-known things.

### 1. Specific / Known Things

```text
The book I bought yesterday is interesting.
The sun rises in the east.
```

### 2. Superlatives

```text
the best student in class
the most expensive phone
```

### 3. Unique Things

```text
the Earth
the moon
the sky
the Prime Minister
the President
```

### 4. Rivers, Oceans, Mountain Ranges

```text
the Padma
the Himalayas
the Pacific
```

### 5. Countries with “United” or Plural Names

```text
the USA
the UK
the Netherlands
```

---

## ❌ When NOT to Use `THE`

Do not use **the** with:

| Category | Correct Example |
|---|---|
| Most country names | I live in Bangladesh. |
| Meals | I had breakfast. |
| Subjects | I study mathematics. |
| Languages | I speak English. |
| Sports | I play cricket. |

---

## 🇧🇩 Common Bangladeshi Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---|---|
| I am doing MBBS. | I am doing an MBBS. |
| I like the music. | I like music. |
| He is good in English. | He is good at English. |
| I go to the school. | I go to school. |
| She is in the home. | She is at home. |
| I saw the moon in sky. | I saw the moon in the sky. |

---

# 📍 Prepositions: `in`, `on`, `at`

## ⏰ Prepositions of Time

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **in** | long periods | in April, in 2026, in summer, in the morning |
| **on** | specific days/dates | on Monday, on May 1st, on my birthday |
| **at** | specific time/moment | at 5 PM, at noon, at midnight, at dawn |

---

## 📌 Prepositions of Place

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **in** | inside / enclosed area | in the room, in Dhaka, in Bangladesh, in the car |
| **on** | surface | on the table, on the wall, on the bus, on the floor |
| **at** | specific point/location | at home, at school, at the bus stop, at the door |

---

## 🧩 Other Important Prepositions

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| by | method / near | I travel by bus. / by the river |
| with | accompaniment | I went with my friend. |
| for | duration / purpose | I waited for 2 hours. |
| from | origin | I come from Bangladesh. |
| to | destination | I go to school. |
| of | belonging | The cover of the book |
| about | topic | I read about history. |
| between | 2 things | between you and me |
| among | 3+ things | among the students |

# 🎭 Section 3: Storytelling — Emotion Expansion

## 🎯 Today's Focus: Emotion Expansion

Emotion makes your story powerful.  
Without emotion, stories sound flat and boring.

> Band 7+ stories should make the reader or listener feel something.

---

## 🎨 5 Levels of Emotion Expansion

### Original Simple Emotion

```text
I was happy.
```

### Level 1: Use a Richer Word

```text
I was delighted.
```

### Level 2: Add Physical Reaction

```text
I was delighted, and a big smile spread across my face.
```

### Level 3: Add Cause + Comparison

```text
I was absolutely delighted when I received the news. A big smile spread across my face, and I felt as though I was walking on air.
```

### Level 4: Rich Vocabulary + Sensory Details

```text
An overwhelming wave of joy washed over me when I received the news. My heart was pounding with excitement, a radiant smile spread across my face, and I felt as though I was walking on clouds.
```

### Level 5: Band 8+ Emotion Description

```text
An overwhelming wave of pure joy washed over me when I received the life-changing news. My heart was pounding furiously with excitement, a radiant smile spread uncontrollably across my face, and tears of happiness welled up in my eyes. I felt as though I was walking on clouds, and for a brief moment, everything else in the world seemed insignificant.
```

---

## 🔑 Emotion Expansion Formula

```text
BASIC:
Subject + was/felt + simple emotion

RICH:
Subject + rich emotion verb + physical reaction + cause + comparison/metaphor + impact
```

---

# 🎤 Section 5: Speaking with Emotion

## IELTS Part 2 Cue Card

Describe a time when you felt really happy.

You should say:

```text
- When it happened
- Where you were
- What caused this happiness
- How it affected you
```

---

## Band 7+ Template

```text
[SETTING]
I'd like to talk about [occasion], which happened [when] in [where]. It's a memory I will cherish forever.

[BUILD-UP]
At the time, I was [what you were doing]. Everything seemed ordinary until [something happened]. I had been [past perfect continuous] for [duration].

[PEAK EMOTION]
The moment I [key event], an overwhelming wave of [emotion] washed over me. My heart [physical reaction]. I felt as though [metaphor]. Tears of [joy/happiness] [action].

[IMPACT]
This experience had a profound impact on me. It taught me that [lesson].

[CONCLUSION]
To this day, whenever I think about that moment, I can still feel [emotion].
```

---

## Sample Band 7+ Answer

```text
I'd like to talk about the day I received my university acceptance letter, which happened on a sunny afternoon in July last year. It's a memory I will cherish forever.

At the time, I was having lunch with my family at our home in Dhaka. Everything seemed ordinary until my phone suddenly buzzed with an email notification. I had been anxiously waiting for this news for over three months, and my heart immediately started racing.

The moment I opened the email and saw the word “Congratulations”, an overwhelming wave of pure joy washed over me. My heart was pounding furiously with excitement, my eyes lit up, and a radiant smile spread across my face. I felt as though I was walking on clouds. Tears of happiness welled up in my eyes, and I leapt up from my chair, hugging my parents tightly. My mother was equally elated, and we celebrated together.

This experience had a profound impact on me. It instilled in me the belief that hard work truly pays off.

To this day, whenever I think about that moment, I can still feel the same rush of joy and gratitude.
```

# 📊 Section 7: IELTS Writing Task 1 — Complete Essay

Today, you will write a full Task 1 essay.

```text
Introduction + Overview + Body 1 + Body 2 = 150 words
```

---

## Full Task 1 Structure

| Paragraph | Purpose | Word Count |
|---|---|---:|
| Paragraph 1 | Introduction | 25 words |
| Paragraph 2 | Overview | 30 words |
| Paragraph 3 | Body 1 | 50 words |
| Paragraph 4 | Body 2 | 45 words |
| **Total** |  | **150 words** |

---

## Full Sample Essay

### Question

```text
The line graph shows the number of international students enrolled in 3 universities A, B, and C from 2010 to 2020.

University A: 2000 → 8000
University B: 3000 → 5000
University C: 4000 → 3000
```

### Introduction

```text
The line graph illustrates the changes in the number of international students enrolled in three universities, A, B, and C, over a ten-year period from 2010 to 2020.
```

### Overview

```text
Overall, it is clearly evident that University A witnessed the most dramatic increase in enrollments, while University C experienced a steady decline. University B showed a moderate upward trend throughout the decade.
```

### Body 1

```text
Looking more closely at University A, the number of international students rose dramatically from approximately 2,000 in 2010 to around 8,000 by 2020, representing a fourfold increase. This upward trend was particularly steep between 2015 and 2020, during which enrollments surged from 4,000 to 8,000.
```

### Body 2

```text
In stark contrast, University C experienced a gradual decline, with enrollments dropping from 4,000 to approximately 3,000. Meanwhile, University B showed a moderate increase, climbing from 3,000 to around 5,000, with the growth being most noticeable between 2013 and 2017.
```
