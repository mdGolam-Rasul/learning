# 📚 Section 1: IELTS Speaking Part 2 Complete Guide

## What Is Speaking Part 2?

IELTS Speaking Part 2 is also called the **Long Turn** or **Cue Card** section.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Duration | 3–4 minutes total |
| Preparation Time | 1 minute |
| Speaking Time | 1–2 minutes |
| Goal | Aim for the full 2 minutes |
| Format | Examiner gives a topic card with 4 bullet points |
| Speaking Style | You speak alone without interruption |

---

## Cue Card Format

### Example Cue Card

```text
Describe a memorable journey you have been on.

You should say:
- Where you went
- Who you went with
- What you did there
- And explain why this journey was memorable
```

You get:

```text
1 minute to prepare
1–2 minutes to speak
```

---

# 🎯 The 1-Minute Preparation Strategy

## Step 1: Read Carefully

**Time:** 10 seconds

Read the topic and all bullet points carefully.

---

## Step 2: Write Key Points

**Time:** 30 seconds

Write short notes, not full sentences.

Example:

```text
Where: Sajek Valley, Rangamati
Who: Family - parents + sister
What: Hiking, photography, local food
Why: First mountain trip, family bonding
```

---

## Step 3: Prepare Opening and Closing

**Time:** 20 seconds

Example:

```text
Opening:
I'd like to talk about my trip to Sajek Valley.

Closing:
This trip truly remains etched in my memory.
```

---

# 🧱 Perfect Part 2 Structure

## Introduction

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- Rephrase the topic
- Give brief context
- Start naturally

---

## Bullet Point 1

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- Direct answer
- Extra details
- Specific information

---

## Bullet Point 2

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- Direct answer
- Supporting details
- Personal connection

---

## Bullet Point 3

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- What happened / what you did
- Actions and experiences
- Descriptive details

---

## Bullet Point 4: Why / Explain

**Time:** 30–40 seconds

This is the most important part.

Include:

- Emotional explanation
- Personal meaning
- Lesson learned
- Final reflection

---

## Conclusion

**Time:** 10–15 seconds

Include:

- Summary
- Final feeling
- Strong closing sentence

# 🎯 Sample Cue Card 1: Describe a Person

## Cue Card

Describe a person who has influenced your life.

You should say:

- Who this person is
- How you know them
- What they do
- And explain why they have influenced you

---

## Band 7+ Sample Answer

I would like to share a truly heartwarming story about my maternal grandmother, who has had a profound impact on my life. She is unquestionably one of the most influential figures I have ever encountered.

My grandmother, whom we affectionately call Nani, is a remarkably wise woman in her late seventies. She is a petite figure with silver hair and sparkling brown eyes. Her warm smile can instantly light up any room, and she remains incredibly sharp for her age.

I have known her my entire life, but our bond strengthened significantly during my childhood summers, when I spent months at her cozy village home in Comilla. Those were truly magical days.

Although she is officially retired, my Nani constantly engages in meaningful activities. She cultivates a beautiful garden, cooks delectable traditional meals, and spends hours reading religious texts. She is also an incredible storyteller.

What makes her truly influential is her unwavering wisdom and resilience. Despite facing numerous hardships, she has always maintained a positive outlook. She has instilled in me the values of perseverance, kindness, and gratitude. I vividly remember one instance when I failed an important exam. Instead of scolding me, she gently said, “Failure is not the end; it is a stepping stone.” That advice has guided me through every challenge.

To this day, my grandmother remains my greatest source of inspiration, and I cherish every moment I get to spend with her.

# 🎯 Sample Cue Card 2: Describe a Place

## Cue Card

Describe a beautiful place you have visited.

You should say:

- Where it is
- When you visited
- What you did there
- And explain why you found it beautiful

---

## Band 7+ Sample Answer

I would like to describe a truly breathtaking place I visited last year: Sajek Valley, nestled in the picturesque Chittagong Hill Tracts. It remains one of the most stunning destinations I have ever explored.

Sajek Valley is located in Rangamati district of Bangladesh, approximately 12 hours from Dhaka. It is a remote mountainous region inhabited by indigenous communities, surrounded by lush green hills and clouds floating below.

I embarked on this journey with my family during the monsoon season of 2024. We meticulously planned our itinerary for a four-day trip, and it exceeded all my expectations.

During our stay, we engaged in various activities. I indulged in hiking through scenic trails, savored authentic local cuisine, and captured countless mesmerizing photographs. One highlight was watching the sunrise from the hilltop, which was absolutely breathtaking.

What truly made Sajek beautiful was the perfect harmony of natural wonders and cultural richness. The rolling green hills, mist-covered mornings, and star-studded skies created a magical atmosphere. The warm hospitality of indigenous people and their vibrant traditions added a deeper dimension to the beauty. I also relished the serene atmosphere because I felt deeply content and completely disconnected from city chaos.

This excursion remains etched in my memory, and I am already planning to revisit it soon.

# 🔥 Master Story Example

## Topic

A Day That Changed My Perspective

---

## Paragraph 1: Hook + Setting + Character

Little did I know that an ordinary Tuesday morning would completely transform my outlook on life. It was a crisp autumn day in late October, with golden sunlight filtering through the towering buildings of bustling Dhaka. The sweet aroma of freshly brewed coffee drifted from nearby cafes, while cheerful morning chatter filled the air. I was heading to an important job interview at a prestigious multinational company. Dressed in formal clothes and clutching my leather briefcase, I felt nervous excitement bubbling inside me. This could be the turning point of my young career.

---

## Paragraph 2: Normal Life + Conflict Appears

Just as I was walking toward the train station, completely absorbed in rehearsing my interview answers, I suddenly heard a loud commotion across the street. My heart skipped a beat as I saw an elderly woman collapsing on the crowded pavement. People simply walked past her, too busy with their own lives to stop. To my dismay, no one was helping. I found myself facing a difficult dilemma: my life-changing interview awaited, but this helpless woman desperately needed assistance. I was torn between pursuing my career dream and following my moral values.

---

## Paragraph 3: Rising Tension + Climax

My mind raced furiously. Apprehensive about being late and overwhelmed by the woman’s suffering, I stood frozen for a brief moment. Tears welled up in the elderly lady’s eyes, and her frail hand reached out for help. That moment changed everything. Without a second thought, I rushed to her side, gently supported her head, and called for an ambulance. “Do not worry, ma’am,” I whispered softly, “help is on the way.” She looked at me with grateful eyes and murmured, “Thank you, my child. May God bless you.” Compassion erased my earlier anxiety.

---

## Paragraph 4: Twist + Resolution

By the time the ambulance arrived and the elderly lady was safely taken to the hospital, I had already missed my interview by 30 minutes. Devastated yet strangely content, I slowly went to the company office, prepared to accept defeat. However, to my astonishment, the interview panel welcomed me warmly. The Director explained, “We witnessed your act of kindness from our office window. Someone with such compassion is exactly who we want.” I stood there speechless, with tears of disbelief in my eyes.

---

## Paragraph 5: Reflection + Lesson

Looking back, this transformative experience has shaped my values and character. It has taught me that true success comes from kindness, not only from career achievements. Helping others has always mattered to me, but this incident deepened my understanding. I will forever cherish this life-changing lesson.

# ✍️ Section 6: Task 2 Opinion Essay Practice

## Essay Question

Some people believe that social media has a negative impact on young people. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

---

## Essay Structure

| Paragraph | Purpose | Time |
|---|---|---:|
| Introduction | Paraphrase + opinion | 5 min |
| Body 1 | Main idea 1 | 12 min |
| Body 2 | Main idea 2 | 12 min |
| Conclusion | Summary + final opinion | 6 min |
| Proofread | Check grammar and vocabulary | 5 min |

```text
Target: 270 words in 40 minutes
```

---

## Sample Outline

### Opinion

Partially agree. Social media has both negative and positive impacts.

### Body 1: Negative Side

- Mental health issues
- Addiction and distraction
- Example: anxiety and fear of missing out

### Body 2: Positive Side

- Educational opportunities
- Global connection
- Example: online learning and networking

### Conclusion

Balance is key. Responsible use can benefit young people.

---

## Sample Introduction

In contemporary society, social media has become ubiquitous, particularly among younger generations. While some individuals firmly believe that platforms like Facebook and Instagram exert detrimental effects on youth, I partially agree with this view. In my opinion, although social media poses significant challenges, its benefits cannot be entirely overlooked.
