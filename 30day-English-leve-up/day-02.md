# 📚 Section 1: Grammar — Continuous Tenses

## ✅ Present Continuous Tense

### Use

Present Continuous Tense is used for:

- Actions happening now
- Temporary actions
- Future plans

### Formula

```text
Subject + am/is/are + Verb+ing + Object
```

### Rules

| Subject | Helping Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I | am | I am studying English now. |
| He / She / It | is | She is working at a hospital. |
| You / We / They | are | They are playing cricket. |

### Examples

```text
I am writing an essay right now.
She is cooking dinner at the moment.
They are discussing the project.
It is raining heavily outside.
We are traveling to Cox's Bazar tomorrow.
```

---

## ✅ Past Continuous Tense

### Use

Past Continuous Tense is used for an action that was happening at a specific time in the past.

### Formula

```text
Subject + was/were + Verb+ing + Object
```

### Rules

| Subject | Helping Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I / He / She / It | was | I was reading a book. |
| You / We / They | were | They were playing football. |

### Examples

```text
At 8 PM last night, I was studying for my exam.
While I was cooking, my brother was watching TV.
They were discussing something important.
I was walking home when it started raining.
```

---

## ⚡ Past Continuous + Simple Past Combination

This structure is excellent for storytelling.

### Formula

```text
Past Continuous + when + Simple Past
```

### Examples

```text
I was sleeping when the phone rang.
She was cooking when the electricity went off.
We were walking in the park when we saw a beautiful bird.
He was studying when his friend called him.
```

---

## ✨ Spelling Rules for `-ing`

| Rule | Example |
|---|---|
| Add `-ing` | play → playing, read → reading |
| Remove `e` + add `-ing` | write → writing, come → coming |
| Double consonant + `-ing` | run → running, sit → sitting |
| `ie` → `y` + `-ing` | lie → lying, die → dying |

# 🎬 Section 3: Storytelling — Setting Expansion

## 🎯 Today's Focus: Setting Expansion

Setting means the background of a story:

```text
Where did it happen?
When did it happen?
What was the environment like?
What was the atmosphere?
```

A strong setting makes your story more vivid, natural, and Band 7+.

---

## 🎨 5 Levels of Setting Expansion

### Original Simple Setting

```text
It was morning. I was at home.
```

### Level 1: Add Time Details

```text
It was a bright Sunday morning. I was at home.
```

### Level 2: Add Weather / Atmosphere

```text
It was a bright and sunny Sunday morning with a cool breeze. I was at home.
```

### Level 3: Add Sensory Details

```text
It was a bright and sunny Sunday morning with a cool breeze. Birds were chirping outside my window, and the smell of fresh tea filled the air. I was at home, relaxing on my comfortable sofa.
```

### Level 4: Add Rich Vocabulary + Continuous Tense

```text
It was an absolutely glorious Sunday morning, with golden sunlight streaming through my windows and a refreshing breeze gently swaying the curtains. Birds were chirping melodiously outside, while the delightful aroma of freshly brewed tea was wafting through the house. I was lounging comfortably on my sofa, thoroughly enjoying the peaceful atmosphere.
```

### Level 5: Band 8+ Setting

```text
It was an absolutely glorious Sunday morning in early spring, with warm golden sunlight streaming through my bedroom windows and a gentle, refreshing breeze swaying the curtains rhythmically. Birds were chirping melodiously outside, creating a symphony of natural sounds, while the delightful aroma of freshly brewed tea was wafting from the kitchen. I was lounging comfortably on my cozy sofa, thoroughly enjoying the serene atmosphere that seemed to promise a perfect day.
```

---

## 🔑 Setting Expansion Formula

```text
BASIC:
When + Where

RICH:
Specific Time + Weather/Season + Sensory Details + Your Activity/State + Atmosphere/Mood
```

---

# 🎤 Section 5: Speaking Practice — Daily Routine

## Task

Describe your typical day in **2 minutes**.

---

## Band 7+ Template

```text
[INTRODUCTION]
I'd like to walk you through my typical day, which is quite hectic but rewarding.

[MORNING ROUTINE]
I usually wake up at _____. The first thing I do is _____. After that, I have breakfast and _____.

[WORK/STUDY]
I then head to _____ where I'm currently _____. During the day, I collaborate with _____ and tackle various tasks. I strive to _____.

[EVENING]
In the evening, I try to unwind by _____. I also spend quality time with my family.

[CONCLUSION]
Overall, my day is quite demanding but I find it extremely fulfilling.
```

---

## Sample Band 7+ Answer

```text
I'd like to walk you through my typical weekday, which is quite hectic but incredibly rewarding.

I usually wake up at around 6:30 AM and start my day with a refreshing cup of tea. After that, I spend about 30 minutes exercising, which energizes me for the day ahead.

At 9 AM, I head to my workplace where I'm currently working as a software developer. Throughout the day, I collaborate with my talented colleagues on innovative projects and tackle numerous technical challenges. I always strive for excellence and pay meticulous attention to detail, which helps me accomplish my goals efficiently. Although my schedule is quite demanding, I find it genuinely fulfilling.

In the evening, I usually unwind by reading novels or watching documentaries, which help me de-stress after a long, exhausting day. I also cherish spending quality time with my closely-knit family over dinner.

Overall, my day is quite hectic, but I'm extremely passionate about what I do, and I wouldn't trade it for anything.
```

# 📊 Section 7: IELTS Writing Task 1 Introduction

## Introduction Formula

```text
The [chart type] [illustrates/shows/depicts] [what data] [in/for/during what period/place].
```

---

## Chart Types Vocabulary

| Chart Type | Rich Synonyms |
|---|---|
| Line graph | line graph, line chart |
| Bar chart | bar chart, bar graph |
| Pie chart | pie chart, pie graph |
| Table | table |
| Process | diagram, process flow |
| Map | map, maps |

---

## Verbs to Use

| Verb | Meaning / Use |
|---|---|
| illustrates | most common and sophisticated |
| depicts | formal and visual |
| demonstrates | analytical |
| presents | neutral |
| provides information about | descriptive |
| compares | for comparison charts |

---

## Example 1: Line Graph

### Question

```text
The line graph shows the population of three cities from 2000 to 2020.
```

### Weak Answer

```text
The graph shows population of three cities.
```

### Band 7+ Answer

```text
The line graph illustrates the changes in population of three cities over a 20-year period, from 2000 to 2020.
```

---

## Example 2: Bar Chart

### Question

```text
The bar chart shows the number of tourists visiting four countries.
```

### Band 7+ Answer

```text
The bar chart depicts the number of international tourists who visited four different countries during a specific period.
```

---

## Example 3: Pie Chart

### Question

```text
The pie chart shows the percentage of energy sources used in a country.
```

### Band 7+ Answer

```text
The pie chart provides information about the proportion of various energy sources utilized in a particular country.
```
