# 📚 Section 3: Storytelling with Dialogue

## Why Dialogue Is Powerful

Dialogue makes stories more alive and natural.

It helps you:

- Show character personality
- Add realism
- Use advanced grammar
- Avoid boring narration
- Make stories memorable
- Demonstrate direct and reported speech

---

# 🎯 Two Types of Dialogue

## Type 1: Direct Speech

Direct speech gives the exact words spoken.

### Format

```text
Character said, "Exact words."
```

### Rules

- Use quotation marks
- Use a comma before the quote
- Start the quote with a capital letter
- Keep punctuation inside the quotation marks

### Examples

```text
My father said, "I'm proud of you, son."
She exclaimed, "What a beautiful day!"
He whispered, "Don't tell anyone."
```

---

## Type 2: Reported Speech

Reported speech explains what someone said without quoting exact words.

### Format

```text
Character said that + reported clause
```

### Tense Changes

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---|---|
| I am happy. | He said that he was happy. |
| I went there. | She said that she had gone there. |
| I will come tomorrow. | He said that he would come the next day. |

### Examples

```text
My father said that he was proud of me.
She mentioned that she had visited Paris before.
He told me that he would come the next day.
```

---

# 💎 Rich Dialogue Tags

| Simple Word | Band 7+ Alternatives |
|---|---|
| said | exclaimed, whispered, muttered, announced, declared, uttered, remarked |
| asked | inquired, questioned, wondered, queried |
| replied | responded, answered, retorted |
| shouted | bellowed, yelled, cried out |
| said quietly | whispered, murmured, mumbled |

---

# 🎬 Dialogue Examples in Stories

## Without Dialogue

My mother was happy when I got the news. She hugged me and we celebrated.

## With Dialogue

The moment I announced the news, my mother’s eyes lit up with joy. Tears welled up as she exclaimed, “I knew you could do it, my dear!” She embraced me tightly, whispering, “All those sacrifices were worth it.” We spent the entire evening celebrating our shared triumph.

---

## Band 8+ Dialogue Story Example

Having waited anxiously for hours, I finally received the life-changing call. My hands trembled as I pressed the phone to my ear.

“Congratulations,” the recruiter announced warmly, “you have been selected for the position.”

For a moment, I stood frozen, unable to process the news. Then, an overwhelming wave of joy washed over me.

“Thank you so much!” I exclaimed, my voice quivering with excitement.

I immediately called my father, who had been eagerly awaiting this update.

“Baba, I got the job!” I said breathlessly.

“I am incredibly proud of you,” he replied, his voice filled with emotion. “Your hard work has finally paid off.”

---

# 🔑 Dialogue Placement Tips

Use dialogue:

- At key moments
- During emotional scenes
- During the climax
- To reveal important information
- To show relationship between characters

Avoid:

- Too many dialogue lines
- Unclear speakers
- Dialogue that does not move the story forward
