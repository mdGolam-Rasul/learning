# 📚 Section 3: Education Speaking Practice

## 🎤 Education Part 1 Questions

### Question 1

**Did you enjoy school when you were younger?**

### Band 8+ Answer

To be honest, I thoroughly enjoyed my primary school years, particularly because of the extracurricular activities that nurtured my cognitive development beyond textbooks. However, my high school experience was more challenging due to the heavy emphasis on rote learning and rigorous examinations. Nevertheless, both phases shaped me significantly and fostered my love for lifelong learning.

---

### Question 2

**What subject did you like most?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Without a doubt, my favorite subject was literature, which I found remarkably enriching. It fostered my critical thinking and broadened my perspective on diverse cultures and human experiences. My literature teacher, a truly charismatic educator, had a profound impact on shaping my appreciation for storytelling.

---

### Question 3

**Is education important in your country?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Absolutely. Education is of paramount importance in Bangladesh. Our society values academic achievement as a pathway to social mobility and economic prosperity. In recent years, government initiatives have improved literacy rates and reduced dropout rates. However, challenges remain, especially regarding educational quality and ensuring true meritocracy in accreditation systems.

# 🎤 Education Part 2 Cue Card

## Cue Card

Describe a teacher who has influenced you.

You should say:

- Who this teacher is
- How you know this teacher
- What this teacher taught you
- Why this teacher influenced you

---

## Sample Answer Structure

### Introduction

I would like to talk about Professor Karim, my high school physics teacher, who profoundly shaped my academic journey.

### Who He Is

Professor Karim is a charismatic teacher in his early sixties, with silver hair and intelligent eyes. He has been teaching for over thirty years and is widely respected among alumni.

### How I Know Him

I first met him in my 11th grade physics class. At first, I found physics difficult and intimidating, but his innovative pedagogy quickly changed my attitude.

### What He Did

Unlike teachers who relied heavily on rote learning, he used real-world examples, hands-on experiments, and thought-provoking discussions. He encouraged us to ask questions and develop critical thinking.

### Why He Influenced Me

What made him extraordinary was his belief in every student. When I failed a mock exam, he did not criticize me. Instead, he said, “Failure is simply feedback.” That changed how I viewed challenges. Under his guidance, I improved academically and developed a more holistic mindset.

### Conclusion

I will always be grateful to Professor Karim because his influence helped shape both my education and my personality.

# 🎤 Education Part 3 Questions

## Question 1

**How has education changed in recent years?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Education has changed significantly in recent decades. Technology integration has transformed both teaching and learning. Online platforms, digital literacy programs, and AI-powered educational tools are now widely used. In addition, pedagogy has shifted from rote learning toward critical thinking and holistic development. For example, many universities in Bangladesh now offer blended learning. However, the digital divide still creates inequality between urban and rural students.

---

## Question 2

**Should governments provide free education?**

### Band 8+ Answer

I strongly support free education, especially at primary and secondary levels. Free education is essential for building true meritocracy and reducing socioeconomic inequality. Countries such as Germany and Finland show that accessible education can produce highly skilled workforces. However, completely free higher education may be difficult for some governments to fund. A practical solution would be free basic education, subsidized university education, and scholarships for talented students.

# ✍️ Section 4: Task 2 Education Essay Practice

## Essay Question

Some people believe that university education should be free for all students, while others argue that students should pay for their own education. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

---

## Planning

### View 1: Free University Education

- Equal access
- Social mobility
- Stronger economy
- Merit-based opportunity

### View 2: Students Should Pay

- Financial responsibility
- Better funding for quality education
- Economic practicality
- Reduced burden on taxpayers

### Opinion

Primary and secondary education should be free, but university education should use a hybrid model with subsidies, scholarships, and reasonable student contributions.

---

## Band 8+ Sample Essay

In contemporary society, the debate over university education funding has intensified globally. While some people firmly support universally free university education, others argue that students should take financial responsibility for their own studies. In my view, although both perspectives have merit, a balanced approach combining state subsidies, scholarships, and student contributions is the most sustainable solution.

Supporters of free university education argue that it promotes true meritocracy and social mobility. When financial barriers are removed, talented students from disadvantaged backgrounds can pursue higher education, reducing socioeconomic inequality. Countries such as Germany and Norway demonstrate that affordable higher education can produce highly literate and skilled workforces. In addition, state-funded education allows cognitive development and academic achievement to depend on ability rather than family income.

However, others believe that students should pay tuition fees because universities need substantial funding to maintain quality. Modern institutions require qualified faculty, research facilities, digital infrastructure, and updated curricula. If education is completely free, governments may struggle to provide sufficient resources. Some also argue that students value education more when they contribute financially. I partially agree with this view. A hybrid model is more practical: scholarships should support low-income and high-achieving students, while those who can afford tuition should make reasonable contributions.

In conclusion, completely free university education promotes equality, but student contributions help sustain quality. Therefore, governments should create a balanced system with public funding, generous scholarships, and affordable fees to ensure both accessibility and excellence.
