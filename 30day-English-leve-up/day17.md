# 📚 Section 1: Event Cue Cards Complete Guide

## Why Event Cue Cards Matter

Event cue cards are important because:

- Around 25% of cue cards are about events
- They connect well with storytelling skills
- They allow use of setting, action, emotion, climax, and resolution
- They are ideal for personal and emotional speaking answers

---

## Common Event Cue Card Topics

- Describe a memorable event
- Describe a wedding or celebration you attended
- Describe a festival in your country
- Describe a day you felt proud
- Describe an event that made you happy
- Describe a childhood event you remember
- Describe a historic event you know about
- Describe a family event
- Describe a sports event you watched
- Describe an event that changed your life

---

# 📋 Event Cue Card Universal Structure

## Introduction

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- “I’d like to talk about...”
- Name of the event
- Emotional hook
- Brief importance

---

## When It Happened

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- Specific date or season
- Your age or life stage
- Context of your life at that time

---

## Where It Happened

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- Location details
- Atmosphere
- 2–3 sensory details

---

## What Happened

**Time:** 45–55 seconds

This is the most important part.

Include:

- Main events in chronological order
- Clear climax
- 2+ dialogue lines
- Rich emotions

---

## Why It Was Memorable

**Time:** 35–40 seconds

Include:

- Emotional significance
- How it changed you
- Life lesson
- Resolution

---

## Conclusion

**Time:** 10–15 seconds

Include:

- Final feeling
- Lasting memory
- Emotional closure

---

# 🎯 Sample Event Cue Card

## Topic

Describe a memorable event from your childhood.

## Band 8+ Sample Answer

I would like to share a truly heartwarming memory from my childhood: my 10th birthday celebration, which remains one of the happiest days of my life.

This event took place on December 15th, when I was just ten years old. At that time, I was a shy and bookish child who had recently moved to a new neighborhood and had not made many friends.

The celebration was held at our cozy family home in Mohammadpur, Dhaka. The living room was decorated with colorful balloons, twinkling fairy lights, and the aromatic fragrance of my mother’s homemade chocolate cake filled the house.

At first, I felt apprehensive because I barely knew anyone in the new area. However, to my astonishment, my parents had secretly arranged for my old school friends to travel three hours from my previous city. When I saw them enter the room, my heart leaped with joy. My best friend Sameer shouted, “We would not have missed your birthday for anything!” We played games, laughed endlessly, and shared stories. During the cake-cutting moment, my mother whispered, “This is just the beginning of many beautiful memories.”

Looking back, this event was much more than a birthday party. It taught me that true friendship can transcend distance and that family love is unconditional. Whenever I feel lonely, I revisit this memory and feel deeply loved.

I will forever cherish this magical day.

# 📚 Section 2: Object Cue Cards Complete Guide

## Why Object Cue Cards Matter

Object cue cards are important because:

- Around 15–20% of cue cards are about objects or things
- They require rich description
- They allow strong use of sensory vocabulary
- They often need emotional or sentimental connection

---

## Common Object Cue Card Topics

- Describe a gift you received
- Describe a useful app or gadget
- Describe your favorite book
- Describe a piece of clothing
- Describe a photograph you love
- Describe an item you lost
- Describe something you want to buy
- Describe a piece of technology you use
- Describe a childhood toy
- Describe something valuable to you

---

# 📋 Object Cue Card Universal Structure

## Introduction

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- “I’d like to describe...”
- Name of the object
- Brief emotional hook

---

## What It Is

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- Physical description
- Color
- Size
- Material
- Texture
- Sensory details

---

## How You Got It

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- When you got it
- Where you got it
- Who gave it to you
- Brief backstory

---

## How You Use It

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- Frequency of use
- Purpose
- How it fits into daily life

---

## Why It Is Special

**Time:** 40–45 seconds

Include:

- Emotional significance
- Memories attached
- Sentimental value
- What it represents

---

## Conclusion

**Time:** 10–15 seconds

Include:

- Future connection with the object
- Overall feeling

---

# 🎯 Sample Object Cue Card

## Topic

Describe a gift you received.

## Band 7+ Sample Answer

I would like to describe a meaningful gift: a leather-bound diary that my late grandfather gave me on my 18th birthday.

The diary is beautifully crafted with a rich brown leather cover, intricate embossed patterns, and cream-colored pages that smell faintly of old books. It has an elegant bronze clasp and feels both portable and valuable.

My grandfather gave it to me during my birthday dinner, two years before he passed away. He placed it carefully in my hands and said, “Write your dreams here; they will find their way to reality.”

I use this diary regularly to write my thoughts, reflections, goals, inspirational quotes, and occasional sketches.

What makes this gift extraordinary is not its material value but its emotional significance. After my grandfather’s passing, it became my most cherished possession. Every page I fill feels like a conversation with him. During difficult times, I re-read my old entries and find strength in his memory.

It is more than a diary. It is a bridge to my grandfather’s spirit and a record of my personal growth.

# 📚 Section 4: Personal Narrative Storytelling

## What Is a Personal Narrative?

A personal narrative is a real-life story where **you are the main character**.

Band 7+ storytelling should feel:

- Specific
- Personal
- Emotional
- Reflective
- Authentic

---

# 🎬 Band 6 vs Band 8+ Storytelling

## Band 6 Style

```text
Generic story that could belong to anyone.
```

## Band 8+ Style

```text
A unique story with specific personal details, real emotions, and reflection.
```

---

# 🎯 5 Elements of Authentic Narrative

## 1. Specific Details

### Weak

I met an old friend.

### Strong

I bumped into Rahim, my best friend from Class 5, whom I had not seen for 12 years, at Baridhara Diplomatic Zone.

---

## 2. Personal Insights

### Weak

I was happy.

### Strong

A wave of nostalgic warmth washed over me. I had not felt this kind of joy since my childhood days playing cricket with him.

---

## 3. Sensory-Emotional Connection

### Weak

I smelled biryani.

### Strong

The aromatic fragrance of biryani instantly transported me to my grandmother’s kitchen, where laughter and love filled every corner.

---

## 4. Authentic Dialogue

### Weak

He said he was happy to see me.

### Strong

His eyes lit up as he exclaimed, “Bro! Kemon acho? It has been a decade!”

---

## 5. Personal Reflection

### Weak

It was a good experience.

### Strong

Looking back, I realize that this chance encounter was not coincidence. It was a gentle reminder that some friendships transcend time and distance.

---

# 🎯 Personal Narrative Structure

```text
Hook
↓
Scene
↓
Characters
↓
Story
↓
Climax
↓
Resolution
↓
Reflection
```

---

# 🔥 Personal Narrative Example

## Topic

A day I felt proud of myself.

## Sample Narrative

My heart was pounding furiously as I stepped onto the auditorium stage, clutching the trembling microphone. Three hundred pairs of eyes were staring at me, expecting me to speak about women’s empowerment at our university’s annual debate.

It was February 21, 2024, International Mother Language Day. The auditorium buzzed with anticipation. Fluorescent lights glared down, and the smell of fresh paint and anxious sweat lingered in the air.

I was a shy 20-year-old with a lifelong fear of public speaking. My supportive professor, Dr. Rahman, stood in the front row, his encouraging smile my only anchor. My parents watched from the balcony.

Three months earlier, I had refused this opportunity. Dr. Rahman had called me to his office and said, “Fatima, your voice matters. Do not let fear silence you.” Those words stayed with me.

As I began speaking, my voice trembled. My palms were sweating. Then I looked at my mother and saw tears in her eyes. Something shifted within me. I stopped reading my notes and spoke from my heart.

By the end, the audience erupted into applause. I had not only completed my speech but also won first place.

That day, I realized vulnerability is not weakness. It is strength. I did not just win a trophy; I reclaimed my voice.

# ✍️ Section 7: Task 2 Problem/Solution Essay

## What Is a Problem/Solution Essay?

A problem/solution essay asks you to explain:

```text
1. The causes or problems
2. The possible solutions
```

---

## Structure

## Introduction

**Length:** 50 words

Include:

- Paraphrase the problem topic
- Acknowledge its seriousness
- Preview your solution approach

---

## Body Paragraph 1: Problems / Causes

**Length:** 90 words

Include:

- Main causes
- Explanation
- Examples
- Cause-effect vocabulary

---

## Body Paragraph 2: Solutions

**Length:** 90 words

Include:

- Realistic solutions
- How they address the causes
- Solution vocabulary

---

## Conclusion

**Length:** 40 words

Include:

- Summary of the problem
- Emphasize solutions
- Optimistic outlook

---

# ✏️ Essay Question

In many countries, young people are spending too much time on social media. What are the main causes of this issue, and what can be done to solve it?

---

## Sample Introduction

In recent years, excessive social media usage among young people has become a pressing global concern. This pervasive issue stems from multiple factors and demands immediate attention through practical solutions from both individuals and society.

---
