# 📚 Section 3: Society Speaking Practice

## 🎤 Society Part 1 Questions

### Question 1

**How has your country changed in recent years?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Bangladesh has undergone profound transformations in recent decades. Primarily, rapid urbanization has dramatically reshaped our demographics, as millions have migrated from rural villages to bustling cities like Dhaka. Additionally, globalization has influenced our cultural heritage by blending traditional values with modern Western lifestyles. Women’s empowerment has also gained significant momentum, with more females entering higher education and the workforce. However, these changes have also exacerbated socioeconomic disparities, creating new challenges for social cohesion.

---

### Question 2

**Do you think people today are more selfish than in the past?**

### Band 8+ Answer

To be honest, this is a nuanced issue. In some ways, yes, because rising individualism, particularly in urban settings, has weakened traditional collectivist values. Busy modern lifestyles and excessive social media engagement have reduced face-to-face community interactions. However, I would not completely agree. Many young people today are actively engaged in volunteer work, climate activism, and social causes, demonstrating remarkable altruism. Essentially, selfishness and altruism coexist in modern society.

---

### Question 3

**Are traditions important in your country?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Absolutely, traditions hold paramount importance in Bangladesh. Our deeply rooted cultural heritage, including festivals like Pohela Boishakh, Eid celebrations, and wedding ceremonies, serves as the backbone of our identity. These traditions foster social cohesion, strengthen family bonds, and bridge the generational gap. However, younger generations are increasingly reinterpreting traditions to align with modern values. Ultimately, traditions should evolve while preserving their essential cultural significance.

# 🎤 Society Part 2 Cue Card

## Cue Card

Describe a change you would like to see in your society.

You should say:

- What the change is
- Why this change is important
- How it could happen
- What impact it would have

---

## Sample Answer Structure

### Introduction

I would like to discuss a transformation I passionately hope to see in Bangladeshi society: achieving true gender equality and empowering women across all socioeconomic levels.

### What the Change Is

Despite significant progress, Bangladesh still faces deeply rooted gender disparities. Women, particularly in rural and marginalized communities, often have limited access to education, employment, and political representation.

### Why This Change Is Important

Gender equality is not merely a moral imperative. It is fundamentally essential for national progress. Countries with greater gender equality often demonstrate stronger economies, better public health, and enhanced social cohesion.

### How It Could Happen

This change requires multifaceted action. Governments must enforce equal pay laws, expand scholarships for girls, and prevent gender-based violence. Schools should include gender sensitivity in curricula, while families must challenge traditional stereotypes.

### Impact

The impact would be profound. Millions of marginalized women would achieve upward social mobility, strengthening the entire social fabric. A society where every girl can study, work, and lead would become more just, productive, and inclusive.

### Conclusion

Achieving gender equality is ambitious but imperative. Through collective effort, society can move toward genuine equity and empowerment.

# 🎤 Society Part 3 Questions

## Question 1

**How has family life changed in recent years?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Family life has undergone fundamental transformations due to multiple interconnected factors. Traditional joint families are increasingly being replaced by nuclear families, especially in urban centers. Rapid urbanization, professional demands, and rising individualism have driven this shift. Furthermore, gender roles have evolved dramatically, with more women pursuing careers and family responsibilities being shared more equally. While these changes offer freedom and opportunity, they have also weakened certain aspects of social cohesion.

---

## Question 2

**Is it important to preserve cultural traditions?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Preserving cultural traditions is of paramount importance, though it must be balanced with progress. Traditions represent accumulated wisdom, shared identity, and social cohesion. For example, Bangladeshi festivals like Pohela Boishakh foster national unity and celebrate cultural heritage. However, traditions must evolve. Harmful practices such as child marriage or gender discrimination should not be preserved. The goal should be thoughtful preservation: keeping what enriches society while reforming what harms it.

---

## Question 3

**What causes inequality in society?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Social inequality stems from multiple intricate factors. Primarily, unequal access to education, which is a major barrier to social mobility, perpetuates socioeconomic disparities across generations. Secondly, structural economic factors such as unequal wealth distribution and limited employment opportunities widen the gap. Discrimination based on gender, ethnicity, or class also marginalizes specific communities. Addressing inequality requires educational scholarships, progressive policies, anti-discrimination laws, and women’s empowerment initiatives.

# ✍️ Section 4: Task 2 Society Essay Practice

## Essay Question

Some people believe that globalization has had a positive effect on society, while others argue that it has damaged traditional cultures. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

---

## Planning

### View 1: Globalization Is Positive

- Economic opportunities
- Cultural exchange
- Global cooperation

### View 2: Globalization Damages Cultures

- Cultural homogenization
- Loss of traditions
- Western dominance

### Opinion

Both views are valid. Globalization brings benefits, but societies must actively preserve cultural heritage.

---

## Band 8+ Sample Essay

In our increasingly interconnected world, globalization’s impact on society remains fiercely debated. While some firmly advocate that globalization has profoundly benefited societies, others contend that it has damaged traditional cultures. In my perspective, although globalization brings significant advantages, it simultaneously threatens cultural heritage, demanding balanced and conscious approaches to preserve diverse traditions.

Proponents of globalization argue that it has generated unprecedented opportunities. Primarily, economic integration has enhanced social mobility, as millions in developing nations, including Bangladesh, have escaped poverty through international trade and employment. Secondly, cultural exchange broadens horizons by exposing individuals to diverse cuisines, art forms, and philosophies. Furthermore, global cooperation on issues such as climate change, pandemics, and women’s empowerment has strengthened social cohesion across borders. Consequently, globalization has democratized knowledge and empowered marginalized communities.

However, critics argue that globalization has led to cultural homogenization. Western values, media, and consumption patterns increasingly dominate global society, eroding unique cultural heritage. Traditional languages, indigenous crafts, and local customs face extinction. In Bangladesh, Western fast food, English-dominant media, and imported fashion have gradually overshadowed traditional practices. This weakens the social fabric. I partially endorse this concern. While globalization undeniably brings benefits, societies must actively preserve cultural identity through education, local media, and government support for traditional arts.

In conclusion, while globalization has enriched societies through economic opportunities and cultural exchange, its threat to traditional cultures demands urgent attention. Therefore, a balanced approach that embraces global integration while actively preserving cultural heritage offers the most sustainable path forward.
