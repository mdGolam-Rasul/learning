# 📚 Section 3: Technology Speaking Practice

## 🎤 Technology Part 1 Questions

### Question 1

**How often do you use technology in your daily life?**

### Band 8+ Answer

To be honest, technology has become almost indispensable in my daily routine. I use it extensively from the moment I wake up until I go to bed. My smartphone helps me check emails, use GPS, complete digital banking through bKash, and stay connected with loved ones. I also rely on cloud-based productivity tools for academic and professional work. However, I consciously manage my screen time to avoid unhealthy dependency.

---

### Question 2

**Do you prefer learning through technology or traditional methods?**

### Band 8+ Answer

I appreciate both approaches. Technology-based learning offers great convenience because I can access lectures, tutorials, and courses from anywhere. Platforms like YouTube and Coursera make learning more flexible and engaging. However, traditional classroom learning provides face-to-face interaction, spontaneous discussion, and better focus. In my opinion, a blended approach that combines both methods is the most effective.

---

### Question 3

**Do you think children today spend too much time on electronic devices?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Yes, I think this is a serious concern. Many children now spend excessive time on smartphones, tablets, and video games. This can reduce physical activity, weaken social skills, damage attention span, and expose them to cyberbullying. Parents should set clear boundaries and encourage outdoor activities, reading, and face-to-face interaction. Technology should support childhood development, not replace it.

# 🎤 Technology Part 2 Cue Card

## Cue Card

Describe a piece of technology that has changed your life.

You should say:

- What it is
- How you got it
- How you use it
- Why it changed your life

---

## Sample Answer Structure

### Introduction

I would like to talk about a transformative piece of technology: my smartphone. It has fundamentally changed the way I study, work, communicate, and manage daily life.

### What It Is

My smartphone is a user-friendly device that combines many functions into one small gadget. It has a high-resolution camera, AI-powered features, useful apps, and access to the internet.

### How I Got It

I bought it two years ago after saving money for several months. Before that, I used a basic phone, which limited my access to digital tools and online learning.

### How I Use It

I use it for emails, virtual meetings, digital banking, GPS navigation, e-commerce, video calls, entertainment, and online learning. It is also my main source of news and productivity.

### Why It Changed My Life

During the COVID-19 pandemic, my smartphone became essential. I attended online classes, completed assignments, and stayed connected with friends and family. It also improved my digital literacy because I learned skills like coding through online tutorials. However, I try to control my screen time to avoid dependency.

### Conclusion

My smartphone is more than a gadget. It is a gateway to learning, communication, and personal growth.

# 🎤 Technology Part 3 Questions

## Question 1

**How has technology changed the way we communicate?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Technology has completely transformed communication. Digital platforms such as WhatsApp, Zoom, and social media have removed geographical barriers and made instant global communication possible. People can now video-call relatives abroad, attend virtual meetings, and send messages within seconds. However, technology has also reduced face-to-face interaction, and emotional depth can sometimes be lost in online communication. Overall, technology has made communication faster, but not always deeper.

---

## Question 2

**Do you think AI will take over human jobs?**

### Band 8+ Answer

AI-powered automation will definitely affect many jobs, especially repetitive work in manufacturing, customer service, and data processing. However, I do not think AI will replace humans completely. Technology often removes some jobs but creates new ones. For example, the smartphone era created app developers, digital marketers, and online business owners. Jobs requiring creativity, empathy, leadership, and complex problem-solving will still need humans. Workers must improve digital literacy to adapt.

---

## Question 3

**Is social media more harmful than beneficial?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Social media has both benefits and drawbacks. On the positive side, it democratizes information, helps small businesses grow, and connects people globally. However, it can also spread misinformation, increase anxiety, expose users to cyberbullying, and create privacy risks. Social media algorithms often prioritize engagement rather than well-being. In my view, social media is not harmful by itself; the problem is excessive and careless use.

# ✍️ Section 4: Task 2 Technology Essay Practice

## Essay Question

Some people believe that the rapid advancement of technology has improved our quality of life, while others argue that it has created more problems than it has solved. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

---

## Planning

### View 1: Technology Improves Quality of Life

- Medical advancements
- Better communication
- Economic opportunities
- Greater convenience

### View 2: Technology Creates Problems

- Mental health issues
- Privacy concerns
- Job displacement
- Digital divide

### Opinion

Both views are valid, but the benefits of technology outweigh the drawbacks if technology is used mindfully and ethically.

---

## Band 8+ Sample Essay

In contemporary society, technological innovation has entered almost every aspect of life. While some people firmly believe that technology has dramatically improved our quality of life, others argue that it has created more problems than it has solved. In my view, although there are legitimate concerns, technology has mainly enhanced human life when used responsibly.

Supporters of technological progress argue that modern innovations have greatly improved living standards. Medical technologies such as AI-powered diagnostics, robotic surgery, and vaccine development have saved millions of lives. Digital communication platforms have also revolutionized the way people connect, allowing instant global interaction. Moreover, e-commerce has improved access to goods and services, while remote work has given employees more flexibility. During the COVID-19 pandemic, technology helped education, healthcare, and business continue despite lockdowns.

However, critics claim that technological disruption has introduced serious problems. Excessive screen time can damage mental well-being, especially among young people. Social media algorithms may spread misinformation, while cybersecurity threats and privacy violations are increasing. Automation may also replace many workers, widening the digital divide between skilled and marginalized groups. Nevertheless, I believe these problems mainly result from misuse rather than technology itself. Governments should enforce data protection laws, schools should improve digital literacy, and individuals should use technology mindfully.

In conclusion, technology has created some challenges, but its benefits in healthcare, education, communication, and economic opportunity are greater. Responsible governance, ethical innovation, and mindful usage can ensure that technology continues to improve human quality of life.
