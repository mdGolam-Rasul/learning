# 📚 Section 3: Health Speaking Practice

## 🎤 Health Part 1 Questions

### Question 1

**Do you exercise regularly?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Yes, I make a conscious effort to exercise at least four times a week. Primarily, I engage in jogging in the morning and occasionally do yoga sessions for mental well-being. Regular physical activity not only boosts my immunity but also rejuvenates my body and mind, reducing the stress of my busy schedule. I believe that maintaining an active lifestyle is essential for long-term health.

---

### Question 2

**What do you do to stay healthy?**

### Band 8+ Answer

To maintain optimal health, I adopt a holistic approach. Firstly, I prioritize a balanced and nutritious diet by eating vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains while limiting junk food. Secondly, I engage in regular physical activity such as swimming and walking. I also prioritize quality sleep and mindfulness to protect my mental well-being. These preventive healthcare habits have improved my overall vitality.

---

### Question 3

**Do you think modern lifestyles are healthy?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Unfortunately, modern lifestyles are often unhealthy, especially in urban areas. Sedentary lifestyles, excessive screen time, junk food consumption, and chronic stress have become common. In Bangladesh, rising rates of diabetes, heart disease, and mental health problems support this concern. However, awareness is increasing, and more people are now embracing fitness, organic food, and mental wellness practices.

# 🎤 Health Part 2 Cue Card

## Cue Card

Describe a healthy lifestyle change you have made.

You should say:

- What the change was
- Why you made the change
- How you implemented it
- How it affected you

---

## Sample Answer Structure

### Introduction

I would like to talk about a transformative health change I made: adopting a regular morning exercise routine about two years ago. This change has deeply improved my physical and mental well-being.

### What the Change Was

Previously, I had a sedentary lifestyle. I spent hours sitting in front of my laptop without enough movement. I often felt tired, gained weight, and experienced anxiety. So, I decided to include 30-minute morning workouts in my daily routine.

### Why I Made the Change

My motivation came from several factors. My medical checkup showed vitamin deficiencies and early signs of high blood pressure. My doctor warned me that without preventive healthcare, I could become vulnerable to chronic illness. My mother’s health problems also became a wake-up call for me.

### How I Implemented It

I started with short 15-minute walks and slowly moved to cardio workouts, yoga, and strength training. I also improved my diet by reducing junk food, eating more nutritious meals, and drinking enough water. I established a better sleep routine to rejuvenate my body.

### Impact

The impact has been remarkable. I have improved my immunity, lost weight, reduced fatigue, and improved my focus. Mentally, I feel calmer and more confident. This change taught me that a holistic approach to health can transform daily life.

### Conclusion

This lifestyle change has been one of the most valuable decisions I have made. I believe health is the foundation of a fulfilling life.

# 🎤 Health Part 3 Questions

## Question 1

**Why do you think obesity is becoming a global problem?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Obesity has become a global problem because of several interconnected factors. Firstly, sedentary lifestyles have become common due to office work, screen time, and limited physical activity. Secondly, junk food is often cheaper and more accessible than nutritious food. Fast-food companies also use aggressive marketing, especially toward young people. In countries like Bangladesh, Western eating habits and urban lifestyles have increased obesity rates. Solving this problem requires government action, public awareness, and personal responsibility.

---

## Question 2

**Should governments do more to promote healthy lifestyles?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Yes, I believe governments should do more to promote healthy lifestyles because public health is a national responsibility. Governments can regulate harmful products by taxing sugary drinks, limiting tobacco use, and banning junk food advertising for children. They can also invest in parks, sports facilities, and public health campaigns. However, the goal should not be to remove personal freedom. Governments should guide people toward better choices while allowing individuals to make their own decisions.

---

## Question 3

**Is mental health as important as physical health?**

### Band 8+ Answer

Absolutely. Mental health is just as important as physical health because the two are deeply connected. Poor mental well-being can lead to physical symptoms such as fatigue, weakened immunity, and chronic illness. Similarly, physical illness can cause anxiety or depression. In Bangladesh, mental health is still stigmatized, which prevents many people from seeking help. Therefore, awareness campaigns, school education, and accessible counseling services are essential.

# ✍️ Section 4: Task 2 Health Essay Practice

## Essay Question

Some people believe that governments should impose strict regulations on unhealthy food and drinks, while others argue that individuals should be responsible for their own health choices. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

---

## Planning

### View 1: Government Should Regulate

- Public health crisis
- Children need protection
- Marketing exploitation

### View 2: Individual Responsibility

- Personal freedom
- Education over regulation
- Cultural choice

### Opinion

Both are needed: regulation for vulnerable groups and education for adults.

---

## Band 8+ Sample Essay

With the alarming rise of obesity epidemics and lifestyle diseases worldwide, the debate over food regulation has intensified. While some argue that governments should impose strict regulations on unhealthy products, others maintain that individuals should take responsibility for their own health choices. In my view, a balanced approach combining targeted regulation and public education is the most effective solution.

Supporters of strict regulation argue that governments must prioritize public health, especially because chronic illnesses place a heavy burden on healthcare systems. Junk food companies often market their products aggressively, sometimes targeting vulnerable groups such as children. Without government intervention, public health may suffer. Sugar taxes, clear food labeling, and restrictions on junk food advertising to minors have shown positive results in several countries. For example, the United Kingdom’s sugar tax helped reduce sugar consumption and encouraged companies to reformulate drinks.

However, others argue that excessive regulation can limit personal freedom and weaken individual responsibility. Adults should be allowed to make informed choices about what they eat and drink. Educational campaigns about nutritional value, cooking skills, and fitness culture may produce better long-term results than strict bans. I believe the best approach is balanced. Children need protection through rules in schools and advertising limits, while adults need education and access to healthier options.

In conclusion, government regulation is necessary to protect vulnerable groups and address public health problems, but individual freedom should also be respected. Therefore, a hybrid strategy involving targeted regulation, awareness campaigns, and accessible healthy choices is the most practical way to improve public health.
