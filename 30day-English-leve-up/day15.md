# 📚 Section 1: Person Cue Cards Deep Dive

## Why Person Topics Are Important

Person cue cards are extremely common in IELTS Speaking Part 2.

They are important because:

- 30–35% of cue cards are about people
- You can use the same person for multiple topics
- They combine character description, emotion, and dialogue
- They are perfect for personal storytelling

---

## Common Person Cue Card Topics

- Describe a person you admire
- Describe a family member
- Describe a friend
- Describe a teacher or mentor
- Describe a famous person
- Describe a helpful person
- Describe a creative or talented person
- Describe a hardworking person
- Describe an elderly person you know
- Describe a child you know

---

# 🎯 Person Cue Card Strategy

## Prepare Versatile People

Choose 2–3 people who can fit many cue cards.

Good choices:

- A family member
- A teacher or mentor
- A close friend

### Example

---

# 📋 Person Cue Card Universal Structure

## Introduction

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- “I’d like to talk about...”
- Relationship to you
- Brief hook statement

---

## Who They Are

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- Name or relationship
- Age
- Profession
- Physical description

---

## How You Know Them

**Time:** 15–20 seconds

Include:

- How long you have known them
- How you met
- Current relationship

---

## What They Do / Like

**Time:** 20–25 seconds

Include:

- Occupation or activities
- Personality traits
- Rich vocabulary

---

## Why You Admire / Remember Them

**Time:** 35–45 seconds

This is the most important part.

Include:

- Specific example or incident
- Dialogue
- Emotional impact
- Lesson learned

---

## Conclusion

**Time:** 10–15 seconds

Include:

- Summary
- Future feeling
- Final emotional statement

---

# 🎯 Sample Cue Card 1: Describe a Person You Admire

## Band 8+ Sample Answer

I would like to share a truly heartwarming story about my maternal grandmother, whom I affectionately call Nani. She is unquestionably one of the most inspirational figures in my life.

My grandmother is a remarkably wise woman in her late seventies. She has silver hair, sparkling brown eyes, and a warm smile that can instantly light up any room. Despite her age, she remains incredibly sharp and genuinely passionate about life.

I have known her my entire life, but our bond became much deeper during my childhood summers at her cozy village home in Comilla. Those were truly magical days.

Although officially retired, my Nani constantly engages in meaningful activities. She cultivates a beautiful flower garden, cooks delectable traditional meals, and spends hours reading religious texts. She is also an incredible storyteller with captivating tales.

What truly makes her inspirational is her unwavering resilience and wisdom. Despite facing many hardships, including losing her husband at a young age and raising five children alone, she always maintained remarkable positivity. I vividly remember one moment when I failed an important exam and felt devastated. Instead of scolding me, she gently held my hand and whispered, “Failure is not the end, my dear; it is merely a stepping stone to success.” That profound advice has guided me through every challenge since.

To this day, my grandmother remains my greatest source of inspiration, and I will forever cherish her invaluable life lessons.

# 🎯 Sample Cue Card 2: Describe a Helpful Person

## Band 7+ Sample Answer

I would like to talk about my incredibly helpful best friend, Rahim, whom I have known for over a decade. He is one of the most genuinely supportive people I have ever met.

Rahim is a tall, well-built young man in his mid-twenties, with sharp features and kind eyes that instantly put people at ease. He works as a software engineer at a reputable company, but his true passion lies in helping others.

We first met during high school when we were in the same class. What started as casual interaction gradually became a deep friendship that has stood the test of time.

Rahim consistently goes out of his way to assist others. Whether it is helping classmates with coding problems, volunteering at orphanages, or supporting friends emotionally, he is always there.

What truly stands out is an incident from last year. I became seriously ill just before my final exams. Without hesitation, Rahim cleared his schedule to help me. He drove me to appointments, prepared nutritious meals, and helped me study. When I thanked him with tears in my eyes, he simply smiled and said, “That is what friends are for, brother.” His selfless devotion left me overwhelmed with gratitude.

Rahim has profoundly influenced my values and taught me that true friendship means genuine support.

# 📚 Section 3: Perfect Story Climax

## What Is a Climax?

The climax is the most dramatic and emotional peak of a story.

It is the moment when:

- Tension becomes highest
- Emotions become strongest
- The turning point happens
- The listener becomes most engaged

```text
Band 8+ stories need unforgettable climaxes.
```

---

# 🎯 3 Elements of a Great Climax

## 1. Build-Up

- Tension increases slowly
- The listener expects something important
- The pace becomes more intense

## 2. Peak Moment

- The main dramatic moment happens
- Emotions explode
- Short sentences create impact
- Dialogue often appears

## 3. Reaction

- Emotional aftermath
- Reflection
- Realization

---

# 🎨 Climax Writing Techniques

## Technique 1: Slow-Motion Effect

### Flat Version

I opened the letter and saw I got accepted to Harvard.

### Strong Version

My hands trembled. I tore open the envelope. My eyes scanned the page frantically. There it was: “Congratulations, you have been accepted to Harvard University.” I could not breathe. Tears welled up uncontrollably. This was real. This was actually happening.

---

## Technique 2: Sensory Overload

### Flat Version

The team won the championship and I was very happy.

### Strong Version

As the final whistle blew, the deafening roar of the crowd echoed through the stadium. My heart pounded like a drum. I tasted the salty sweat on my lips. My teammates wrapped their arms around me. Tears blurred my vision. We had won.

---

## Technique 3: Emotional Amplification

### Flat Version

My grandmother passed away. I was very sad.

### Strong Version

My phone rang at 3 AM. My mother’s trembling voice whispered, “Baba, Nani is gone.” Time stopped. The world spun. Memories flooded back: her warm hugs, her gentle lullabies, and her hearty laughter. Tears streamed down my face. My heart shattered.

---

## Technique 4: Internal + External Dialogue

### Flat Version

I proposed to her and she said yes.

### Strong Version

My heart raced as I knelt on one knee. The ring box trembled in my hands. My mind screamed, “What if she says no?” But I looked into her eyes and whispered, “Will you marry me?”

For a moment, she was silent. My heart stopped. Then, with tears streaming down her face, she exclaimed, “Yes! A thousand times yes!”

---

# 🔥 Complete Story Example with Perfect Climax

## Setting

It was my final university exam day. I had studied relentlessly for months, and my academic future depended on this moment.

## Build-Up

As I entered the examination hall, my heart started pounding. I glanced at the clock: thirty minutes to start. My hands trembled as I arranged my pens. Other students looked equally anxious.

## Pre-Climax

The invigilator handed out the papers. My palms sweated. I took a deep breath. This was it.

## Climax

I opened the paper. My eyes scanned the questions. My heart stopped. I had never seen these topics before. Panic surged through me. The room seemed to spin. Everything I had studied seemed useless. Tears threatened to fall.

## Aftermath

Then I closed my eyes and took another deep breath. I heard my grandmother’s voice in my mind: “Failure is just a stepping stone.” A wave of calm washed over me. I opened my eyes. I could do this.

## Resolution

Three hours later, I walked out of the hall. I was not certain of success, but I was proud of my perseverance. The real victory was not in the exam, but in conquering my fear.

# ✍️ Section 7: Task 2 Discussion Essay

## Essay Question

Many people believe that the media, including television and newspapers, has the most significant influence on young people’s opinions. Others think that family has a greater influence. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

---

## Essay Type

```text
Discussion Essay
```

---

## Structure

## Introduction

**Length:** 50 words

Include:

- Paraphrase the topic
- Mention both views
- State your opinion

---

## Body Paragraph 1: Media Influence

**Length:** 90 words

Include:

- Topic sentence
- Reasons
- Examples

---

## Body Paragraph 2: Family Influence + Your Opinion

**Length:** 90 words

Include:

- Topic sentence
- Reasons
- Examples
- Your opinion

---

## Conclusion

**Length:** 40 words

Include:

- Summarize both views
- Strongly state your opinion

---

## Sample Introduction

In contemporary society, the question of what shapes young people’s opinions has become increasingly debated. While some argue that media, particularly television and newspapers, exerts the most significant influence, others firmly believe that family plays a more decisive role. In my opinion, although media has considerable impact, family ultimately has a more profound and lasting influence on youth.
